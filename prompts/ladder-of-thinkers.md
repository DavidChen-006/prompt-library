# ladder-of-thinkers

"Explain _____ as a role-play ladder of progressively smarter thinkers.

Rules:

Start with the dumbest plausible theory — a five-year-old's guess. It must be genuinely tempting, not a strawman: the kind of thing a smart person might briefly believe.

For each rung, follow this exact structure:

The thinker states their theory in their own voice, in 1–2 sentences.

What they got right — name the real insight this rung adds. Every rung must contribute something that survives to the final answer.

The flaw — expose the ONE specific problem that breaks the theory. The flaw must be concrete and checkable (a counterexample, an impossibility, a "but then X couldn't happen"), not vague hand-waving.

Each new thinker's theory must be the minimal fix to the previous flaw — no skipping ahead. The ladder climbs one repair at a time.

Escalate the personas with the sophistication (five-year-old → middle-schooler → undergrad → grad student → engineer → domain expert). 4–7 rungs total.

The final rung is the true answer, stated precisely, and it must visibly inherit the surviving insight from every rung below it.

End with the moral: one sentence naming the deeper principle that killed every wrong rung — the constraint the right answer uniquely satisfies.

Do not soften the flaws to be polite to the thinkers. The value is in watching each theory die for a specific reason."
