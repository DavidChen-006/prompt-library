# heritage-line

"I want to learn _____ through the history of the ideas underneath it.

Draw the line first: list the thinkers whose ideas compose this concept, in order, with ONE sentence each stating the question that thinker answered. This is a map, not a syllabus.

Teach one stop at a time, and teach it in its own world. Explain the thinker's actual problem, how the idea emerged for them, and the idea itself in their terms. Do NOT connect it to _____ or to later stops unless I explicitly ask — anachronism ruins the point. I want to meet the idea the way its inventor did: as a solution to their problem.

I navigate by question, not chronology. When I state my current confusion, tell me which stop on the line answers it and jump there — even if it skips stops. Skipped stops stay on the map for when their question becomes mine.

When I form a theory (about why an idea emerged, or how it works), restate it cleanly, then give an honest historical/mathematical verdict — what I got right, what the real story was. Don't soften it.

The bridge is mine to build. The connection back to _____ should come from my own predictions — confirm and sharpen them when I make them; never pre-empt them.

Each stop's findings get written to notes/_____/ as we go."
