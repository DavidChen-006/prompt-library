# tradeoff-construction

"I'm evaluating _____ (option or options). Don't tell me if it's good or bad — that's not a real property of anything.

First, help me construct my axes: interview me briefly if needed, then list the 4–6 dimensions that actually matter for my situation (e.g., scale, rate of change, who needs access, cost tolerance, failure modes I can't accept). Force me to rank them.

For each option, map its trade-offs along those axes specifically. For every advantage, name the corresponding limitation it purchases — no free lunches. If an advantage doesn't cost anything, be suspicious and look harder.

Show me where each option's assumptions align with my axes and where they reverse.

Conclude not with 'X is better' but with: 'X wins if these axes dominate for you; Y wins if those do' — and tell me which one my stated ranking implies."
