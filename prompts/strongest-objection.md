# strongest-objection

Give me the strongest objection to this claim or thesis — the one its most
informed, good-faith critic would make, not a strawman. State the objection
in its most persuasive form, name the evidence or reasoning that powers it,
and specify exactly what would have to be true for the objection to win.
Then, separately, say what would falsify the original claim outright. Do not
resolve the tension or reassure me; leave the objection standing at full
strength.
